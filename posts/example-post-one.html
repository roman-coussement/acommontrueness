On Building in Public

Building in public has become something of a buzzword in the creator economy, but there's real substance behind the trend. The idea is simple: share your process, your struggles, and your learnings as you work‚Äînot just the polished outcomes.

Why the process matters

When we only share our wins, we create an illusion of effortless success. That does a disservice to everyone. Newcomers feel inadequate. Experienced builders feel isolated in their struggles. The truth is that every meaningful project involves false starts, dead ends, and moments of doubt.

"The best way to predict the future is to create it‚Äîand the best way to create it is to share how you're creating it."

What to share

You don't need to document every minute. The most valuable updates are the ones that capture a genuine moment: a breakthrough, a mistake you learned from, or a question you're still wrestling with. These posts resonate because they're honest.

Start small. A weekly update. A thread when something interesting happens. The consistency matters less than the authenticity. Your audience will grow when they sense you're building something real‚Äîand that you're willing to show the messy parts.
